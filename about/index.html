    History

    Start

    I started in software development when I was about 15 years old. A talented friend Arturo Nava supported me in my growth with his tutoring and advice; he taught me what I needed about programming languages, relational databases, how a computer works, its components, etc… The most important thing he taught me was to learn in an autonomous way, not depending on any institution to be able to do what I wanted to do. In this way, despite the bad education of my native country, I managed to enter one of the most powerful modern industries! Over time, I started to learn more from technical books, posts, documentations, online courses and personal practices.

    First job as Front-End developer

    My first job was as Front-End developer in a company creating software for other companies and own projects. This is where my first opportunity to enter the software world started. I like to remember this time because I used pictures of my partner and initials in my development examples hahah.

    First job as Full-Stack developer

    My first job as a Full-Stack developer at Fyld Inc was a strong shock to everything I knew, it was when I started to learn more about how microservices worked in production projects. Thanks to this job, I got to know more about everything related to cryptocurrencies, BlockChain and so on. Since my arrival, we have been driving interest in potential customers and we have partnered with several others.

    Fall-over

    In my first job as a Full-Stack developer at Fyld Inc I made one of my biggest mistakes. It was when I made the mistake of confusing relationship problems at work with work problems. I argued with my technical leader, who was my own instructor and best friend Arturo Nava. And so, confused by my position in the company, I decided to resign; which was one of the worst mistakes I made in my career. After that, I was unemployed for about a year and a half due to my limited experience. Time I had to learn both from the mistake I made, and to study much more about the industry and not be another Junior with my arms crossed.

    Renaissance!

    After a year and a half without getting a stable job, I managed to join YoFio as Golang Back-End Developer; company that would give me the opportunity to grow and help them grow. This is the company where I managed to learn more unknown things, reinforce previous knowledge and above all how incredible teamwork is. Since then I consider myself a Team Player, reinforced in all my knowledge.
